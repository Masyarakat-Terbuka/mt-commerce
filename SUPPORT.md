# Support

There are a few ways to get help with mt-commerce, depending on what you need.

---

## Asking questions

For general questions — how to do something, how something works, advice on architecture or deployment — please use **[GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/masyarakat-terbuka/mt-commerce/discussions)**. Discussions are public, searchable, and let other community members benefit from the answers.

You can ask in Bahasa Indonesia or English. Both are welcome.

Before asking, a quick search of existing discussions and issues often turns up an answer.

---

## Reporting bugs

If you have found a bug, please open a **[GitHub Issue](https://github.com/masyarakat-terbuka/mt-commerce/issues)**. The issue template will guide you through the information that helps us reproduce and fix the problem.

A good bug report includes:

- What you did
- What you expected to happen
- What actually happened
- The version of mt-commerce you are running
- Steps another person can follow to reproduce the issue

---

## Requesting features

For feature ideas, please open a **[discussion](https://github.com/masyarakat-terbuka/mt-commerce/discussions)** first rather than an issue. This gives the community a chance to talk through the idea before it becomes a concrete proposal.

If a discussion leads to consensus that a feature should be built, it can be turned into an issue and added to the roadmap.

---

## Reporting security issues

Security vulnerabilities should not be reported publicly. Please follow the process in [`SECURITY.md`](./SECURITY.md).

---

## What this is not for

A few requests fall outside what the project can help with directly:

- **Personal account or order help** — if you are a shopper trying to find an order, you need to contact the shop you bought from, not this project.
- **Custom development requests** — the project does not take on commissioned work. Independent agencies and developers can help; the community will list partners as it grows.
- **Direct messages to maintainers** — please use public channels so others can benefit from the answers and so the load is shared.

---

## Response times

Response times depend on the volunteers and contributors available at the moment. We aim to respond to questions and bug reports within a few days, though it can take longer at times. Patience is appreciated.

The fastest way to get a question answered is often to phrase it well, share the relevant code or configuration, and describe what you have already tried.
